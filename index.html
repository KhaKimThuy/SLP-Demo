Abstract

Recent advances in Sign Language Production (SLP) highlight denoising diffusion models as promising alternatives to traditional autoregressive methods. Most existing approaches follow a two-stage pipeline that encodes sign motion into discrete latent codes, often sacrificing spatio-temporal fidelity and requiring gloss annotations or complex codebooks. Transformer-based models aim to simplify this but often produce over-smoothed, unnatural motions. We introduce Sign Language Production with Scale-Aware Modulation SignSAM, a novel single-stage, gloss-free SLP framework that directly synthesizes motion in continuous space, preserving fine temporal details. At its core is a Spatio-Temporal U-Net that learns compact temporal features by jointly downscaling frames and feature dimensions, reducing computational cost. To further enhance temporal precision, we propose a Timing Scale-Aware Modulation module that fuses multi-scale temporal features for better motion coherence. Experiments on PHOENIX14T and How2Sign show that SignSAM achieves state-of-the-art fluency, accuracy, and naturalness, offering an efficient and expressive solution for SLP.
